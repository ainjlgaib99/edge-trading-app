PASSING EVALS
Topstep · Apex · Lucid — how to not blow it
The eval is not testing if you can make big money. It is testing if you can follow rules consistently. That is a completely different game than just trading.
⚠️ #1 Reason Evals End
Hitting the daily loss limit. A $50K account might have a $2,000 daily loss limit. Lose $1,500 and keep trading, lose another $600 — failed. Walk away when you're down 50–60% of the daily limit. That's your personal daily stop, not the firm's limit.
1
Trade smaller than you're allowed. The eval lets you trade 5 contracts. Trade 1. Your job is to prove consistency, not max P&L.
2
Never hit the daily loss limit. Set your own personal daily stop at 50–60% of the firm's limit and respect it like a hard rule.
3
Take your time. Most firms require 10–15 minimum trading days. There is no rush. Take 2–3 high quality trades per week. Slow and clean.
4
Treat every day as independent. A bad Monday does not affect Tuesday. The market doesn't owe you a recovery. Start fresh.
5
Most people fail in the first week by trying to make it all back after a loss. You will not do this. You walk away after 2 losses and come back tomorrow.
